NanoTechnology
in Medicine

"Nanotechnology is an idea that
most people simply didn't believe"
Ralph Merkle

Nanotechnology in medicine is a newly discovered concept that is soon to revolutionize the future of medicine. Nanotech has targeted diseases that modern medicine does not have a cure for, for example cancer. You might be wondering why is nanotech different from modern medicine and what makes it so powerful? Based on research nanotech particles are created with substances, light, heat, etc. to repair damaged cells whereas modern medicine cannot deliver drugs as such. In addition particles are programmed to go to designated cells in our bodies to treat certain cells which make it so much more different than having swallowed a tablet. Therefore, nanotechnology in medicine could possibly make our futures more efficient.

Vocabulary

  • Nanoparticles: microscopic particle
  • Nano: a measurement
  • Nanotech: the use of matter on an atomic, molecular, and supramolecular scale for industrial purposes

    • FAQ

    What is nano-technology? How can it be applied to Pre-med?

    • Nanotechnology is “Nanotechnology is science, engineering, and technology conducted at the nanoscale, which is about 1 to 100 nanometers” based on Nano.gov. Nanotech is applied to the field of pre-med because of the theory that nanomedicine can better cure diseases with its different features. For example “the science and technology of diagnosing, treating, and preventing disease and traumatic injury, of relieving pain, and of preserving and improving human health, using molecular tools and molecular knowledge of the human body.” Based on NCBI

    When/who developed this theory ?

    • Nanotechnology was developed by physicist Richard P Feynman on Dec 29, 1959. It wasn’t unit 1981’s until modern nanotechnology began due to the lack of tools to magnify a nano. Nano.gov

    How does it function?

    • Nanobots have an algorithm/ programmed that is set to go to a designated area in the body, and release nanoparticles. Nanoparticles are specifically designed to treat a certain disease and so when in the destination they fight off or repair the cells. citation

    How is it better than medicine?

    • Nanoparticles can deliver substances like heat, light whereas modern drugs are made from solid/liquid substances. Nanobots are also specifically programmed to target certain cells in the body.

    Timeline

    NanoMedicine in the Past

  • Nanotech was discovered by Richard P Feynman and the possibilities of nanomedicine then occurred.
  • Feynman thought of this idea when his friend Albert R Hibbs mentioned the idea of swallowing mini surgeons to get procedures done
  • In 1968 Eric Dexler brought up a theory of “cell repairing machines”
  • In 1996 Robert Freitas released a book about nanomedicine in detail called Nanomedicine, also known as one of the great nanotech books at the time
  • The book The Nanotechnology Revolution is said to have talked about the first drug delivery using nano-particles
  • The further investigation of nanoparticles, and better turning substances into a nano size started in the 1990’s

    • NanoMedicine in the Present

  • Research laboratories began to open to conduct and operate research within this field. Some companies/ research centers include: NNI (National Nanotechnology Initiative), IWGN (The Interagency Working Group on Nanotechnoloy), etc.
  • NNI was funded by congress for its research and development
  • Colleges and universities started making this a global research in upper level schools
  • Target for nanomedicine in this era is to cure cancer, cardiovascular, and neurodegenerative diseases, as well as other illnesses
  • Clinical trials to further understand nanomedical capabilities and develop cures
